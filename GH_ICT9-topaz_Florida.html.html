The month of March is celebrated as the Fire Prevention Month in order to remind the public of the importance of fire safety. It is usually one of the warmest months in the Philippines, and the weather and humidity reach their peak levels throughout the country.
Avoid Fires!

"never leave your kitchen unattended whilst cooking"
 Any stove should be turned off if you can't watch it, and especially if it's a gas stove. A gas leak can cause an explosion.

"inspect your heating sources"
 Regular inspections of your heating systems are essential to identifying potential problems and resolving them to improve system efficiency.

"store any flammable products correctly"
 There are several reasons why it's important to store flammable liquids safely. First, by keeping these substances away from potential sources of fire, we can reduce the risk of a serious blaze breaking out. Second, using these substances in a responsible way can help prevent them from adding fuel to any existing fires. Finally, keeping flammable liquids in a cool, dry place will also help them last longer and reduce the chances of them spontaneously combusting.

"maintain/manage your wires"
 Electric currents carried by wiring, switches, plugs, and appliances can generate heat. If too much heat builds up, it can start fires. To help prevent electrical fires, be aware of how to use and store plugs and electrical cords.

"keep a fire extinguisher or blanket around at all times!"
 Fire extinguishers and fire blankets can be helpful tools in protecting people and property from fire. Fire extinguishers can put out a small fire, and fire blankets can be used to smother or extinguish fires in hard-to-reach places. Many fire blankets can be used for people on fire.td>
